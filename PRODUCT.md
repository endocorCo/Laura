# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, served equally by the same single-page site:

- **Prospective clients**: women looking for a professional hairstylist/makeup artist for color changes, event styling, bridal makeup, or eyebrow/lash work. They land on the site to see real work (gallery, before/after), build trust, and book an appointment.
- **Aspiring stylists**: beauty professionals interested in Laura's in-person courses to level up their technique. They visit to evaluate her credibility as an instructor and find course details.

## Product Purpose

A one-page portfolio and booking funnel for Laura Zapién Studio (hair artist & makeup artist, Papalotla, Estado de México). Success means a visitor trusts Laura's craft within seconds of landing, browses real work without friction, and either books a appointment via WhatsApp/contact form or inquires about the course.

## Brand Personality

Elegant and warm. Approachable luxury: feminine, soft, hand-crafted, never cold or corporate. Confident but never flashy. Think "atelier" not "agency."

## Anti-references

Must not look AI-generated or template-SaaS. Specifically avoid:
- Identical card grids with icon-in-circle + heading + paragraph, repeated endlessly.
- Generic scroll-fade-in animations applied uniformly to every section just because GSAP is available.
- Stock-photo-grid Instagram sections, hero-metric blocks, side-stripe borders, gradient text.
- Anything that reads as a beauty-salon site builder template rather than a bespoke studio site.

## Design Principles

- Real work over decoration: photos of Laura's actual clients and results carry more weight than icons, stock imagery, or ornamental UI.
- Editorial restraint on mobile: lists and grids should read like a printed lookbook or menu, not a dashboard of cards.
- Motion with intent: animate only where it clarifies content or adds a felt sense of craft (e.g. the before/after slider); never animate by default just to fill scroll-triggers.
- Warmth through typography and color, not through gimmicks: the Playfair Display + Manrope pairing and rosegold/champagne palette already carry the "elegant and warm" personality; new additions should reinforce that, not compete with it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice: sufficient color contrast (note: footer social icons previously failed this and were fixed), legible text sizes, respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animation work. No additional specific requirements identified.
